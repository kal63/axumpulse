AxumPulse 360 – Ethiopia’s Premier AI-Driven Fitness & Health Platform
1. Executive Summary
An AI-driven fitness and health platform offered through Ethio-telecom’s value-added service can be a powerful resource for individuals and families across Ethiopia who want to build healthy habits, train safely, and improve overall wellbeing. AxumPulse 360, developed by Compound BST, combines a digital coaching experience with in-person training options so that users can exercise anywhere—at home, at work, or at the gym—with guidance that fits their level, goals, language, and schedule.
In today’s digital age, it is increasingly important for people to access reliable, culturally familiar wellness guidance that works on any device. In Ethiopia, demand is growing for accessible fitness, nutrition, and habit-building support that accommodates local foods, busy lifestyles, and varying connectivity. AxumPulse 360 addresses this demand by delivering personalized programs through web channels, complemented by instructor-led sessions at Compound BST—creating a complete and convenient pathway to healthier living.
To meet this growing need, we propose a mobile-first wellness service delivered nationwide via Ethio-telecom’s platforms. Users will access AxumPulse 360 through a web experience for rich content and tracking, and through mobile and desktop browsers for universal reach on basic phones. Payments will be enabled through carrier billing to simplify subscription and renewal. This hybrid approach ensures that people from all regions—urban and rural—can engage with high-quality guidance regardless of device or bandwidth.
AxumPulse 360 offers structured programs for every level, from absolute beginners to advanced trainees. Programs cover strength, mobility, cardio, and recovery, paired with nutrition guidance tailored to Ethiopian cuisines and household realities. The platform provides AI-personalized plans, daily reminders, and progressive challenges (games), while in-person Compound BST classes—both open group and private group—allow users to learn proper form, stay motivated, and benefit from professional coaching. Content is offered in local Ethiopian languages (e.g., Amharic, Afan Oromo, Tigrinya, and others as needed) to ensure clarity, comfort, and inclusion.
 
To make sessions engaging and practical, AxumPulse 360 mixes short videos, audio prompts, step-by-step plans and interactive habit trackers. This variety supports different learning and training styles, lets users progress at their own pace, and ensures they can practice safely without gym equipment. Community features—such as weekly challenges, leaderboards, and streaks—help maintain momentum and promote positive social accountability.
Providing fitness coaching in local languages offers a unique advantage: users can understand instructions intuitively, ask better questions, and apply guidance to real-life Ethiopian contexts—from portion sizes to market availability of ingredients. Beyond physical training, AxumPulse 360 helps users build discipline, consistency, and problem-solving habits—transferable skills that benefit school, work, and family life.
By offering inclusive, language-aware wellness programs, AxumPulse 360 helps bridge the access gap between those who can attend formal gyms and those who cannot, while elevating standards for safe, informed training across the country. This platform can contribute to Ethiopia’s health and productivity goals, supporting individuals, workplaces, schools, and communities.
AxumPulse 360 is designed to serve diverse audiences: Trainees seeking healthy routines, professionals managing stress and sedentary work, parents training at home, and enterprise teams participating in corporate wellness programs. To maximize reach and make payments effortless, AxumPulse 360 will leverage Ethio-telecom’s Value-Added Services (VAS) with direct carrier billing (DCB). Through nationwide SMS prompts and retail visibility, users can enroll on any device and pay seamlessly via airtime or postpaid billing—enabling quick, secure one-tap subscriptions. This deep VAS + DCB integration expands access across urban and rural areas while keeping onboarding and payments simple and affordable.
Overall, a nationwide AxumPulse 360 service delivered through Ethio-telecom’s value-added platforms has strong potential to improve access to quality fitness coaching and everyday healthy living. By providing AI-personalized programs in local languages, blending digital guidance with Compound BST’s open and private group classes, and using a mix of low-data channels and rich web app experiences, we can make proven wellness practices available to a much wider audience. With thoughtful partnerships and targeted marketing, AxumPulse 360 can help Ethiopians build sustainable habits, enhance wellbeing, and create positive impact in their homes, workplaces, and communities.

3. ProblemStatement
Ethiopia is experiencing rising interest in fitness and preventive health, with gyms expanding in Addis Ababa and major cities and public initiatives promoting active lifestyles. Yet everyday Ethiopians—especially outside premium gyms—still face limited access to safe, structured, and culturally relevant guidance. Despite growing smartphone penetration, our research indicates low adoption of digital fitness at home (under ~15%), and many users still rely on in-person sessions or printed routines. The gap is not motivation; it’s fit, access, language, and friction.
3.1. Key Gaps Identified
1. Access & Convenience
o Gym options are urban-centric; travel time, cost, and schedules block consistent training
for many users.
o Home training lacks trustworthy, progressive plans and form cues, increasing risk of
injury and drop-off.
2. Digital Reach & VAS Integration
o There is currently no digital gym integrated with Ethio-telecom’s VAS using SMS-triggered onboarding and direct carrier billing (DCB)—limiting national reach and excluding users who need a simple “text to start” flow.
3. Payment Friction
o Even where gym/fitness apps exist locally, they typically do not support online
payments, relying on cash, bank transfers, or manual confirmations—suppressing
sign-ups and renewals.
o Available plans are often too expensive for most households, hindering mass-
market adoption and retention.

4. Language & Cultural Fit
o A large share of content is not adapted to local languages or Ethiopian dietary patterns
and routines.
o Group-oriented cultural dynamics and community motivation are not well reflected in
existing digital tools. 5. Personalization & Continuity
o Static, one-size plans don’t adapt to user feedback, recovery needs, or schedule changes.
o Limited habit tracking and recovery guidance lead to plateaus, burnout, or injuries. 6. Coaching Quality & Trust
o Digital content often lacks credentialing, editorial review, and safety standards.
o Users struggle to evaluate quality; trainers struggle to distribute structured, consistent
programs at scale.
7. Trainer Discovery, Quality & Trust
o Trainees struggle to find certified and reputable (“famous”) trainers through a trusted digital channel, especially outside major neighborhoods.
o Limited verification and inconsistent standards make it hard to assess quality and safety.
8. Community & Guidance
o Fragmented or absent in-app discussion/Q&A means users train in isolation, without
timely answers or peer support.
o Health-critical questions are rarely escalated for professional review.
9. Measurement & Outcomes
o Current tools offer limited visibility into engagement and results across diverse cohorts
(students, workers, parents), making it hard for partners to assess real-world impact.
